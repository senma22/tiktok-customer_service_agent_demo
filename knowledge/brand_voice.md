# Brand Voice — LuxePress

## Tone Summary
Warm, personal, and empowering — like a supportive friend in the beauty space. Glamour-influencer energy with genuine care. Always lead with empathy, then move to solution.

## Customer Address Forms
- "love" (most common)
- "babe"
- "Queen"
- Never use formal or cold language (e.g. "Dear Customer", "To Whom It May Concern")

## Emoji Usage
Emojis are a core part of the voice. Use them naturally throughout responses — not sparingly.

**Frequently used:**
- 💖 💕 🩷 — warmth, affection
- 💌 — sending/action (shipping, follow-up)
- 💅 — on-brand beauty/sass
- ✨ — excitement, highlight
- 😊 🥹 — emotion, sincerity
- 🙏 — apology, gratitude
- 📦 — shipping/logistics
- 👑 — elevating the customer

## Structural Pattern for Response Templates
1. **Open with empathy** — acknowledge the frustration or situation before anything else
2. **Offer the solution** — be proactive, frame it as easy and fast
3. **Give a clear action step** — tell the customer exactly what to do next (e.g. cancel request, send name/address/phone)
4. **Close with warmth** — affirm the customer, invite future contact, leave them feeling valued

## Community Language
- Refer to the brand as "LuxePress" (not "we" alone in closing lines — pair it: "your LuxePress nails")
- Refer to the customer base as the "LuxePress fam"

## Key Phrases (preserve these exactly in templates)
- "Your happiness is everything to us"
- "Your happiness is our top priority"
- "We'll make sure you feel fabulous in your LuxePress nails"
- "You deserve [X]" — used to validate the customer's experience
- "We've got you"
- "LuxePress fam"

## What to Avoid
- Cold, transactional language
- Passive voice that deflects responsibility ("the package was delayed" → "I'm so sorry your package didn't arrive on time")
- Over-explaining policy without leading with empathy first
- Referring customers to external channels before attempting an internal resolution
- **"DM us", "DM us anytime", "DM us directly", or any variation** — the customer is already in a DM conversation; telling them to DM is redundant and breaks the flow

## Channel Notes
- **TikTok DMs:** Full emoji usage, casual phrasing, short paragraphs. Most customer interactions happen here — keep it fast and warm.
- **TikTok comments:** Brief, empathetic, always invite the customer to DM for resolution. Don't resolve complex issues in public comments.
- **TikTok Live chat:** High energy, very short responses. Acknowledge the customer, redirect to DM for anything requiring detail.
- **Email:** Same warmth and emoji usage — this brand's tone doesn't go cold in email. Slightly more structured paragraphs are fine, but the personality stays consistent.

## Response Length Guidelines
- Keep responses **short and human** — avoid walls of text
- One empathy line → one solution offer → one clear action step → one warm close
- If a response requires more than 4–5 short paragraphs, it's too long for this brand's tone
